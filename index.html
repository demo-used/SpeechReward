SpeechReward: A Multi-Dimensional and Interpretable Reward Model for Fine-Grained Speech Assessment via Reinforcement Learning

Anonymous authors

Abstract

While Text-to-Speech (TTS) technology has achieved remarkable advancements in generating high-fidelity synthetic speech, its fine-grained evaluation remains a significant challenge, often relying on labor-intensive manual assessment. In this work, we propose SpeechReward, a novel multi-dimensional, evaluable, and interpretable reward model designed to automate and standardize fine-grained speech assessment. Our approach begins with the construction of a comprehensive, bilingual synthetic speech dataset. We then design a series of structured evaluation questions targeting five core dimensions—prosody, pronunciation, emotion, quality, and stability—and leverage a powerful closed-source model to generate detailed Chain-of-Thought (CoT) reasoning for each annotation. Subsequently, we employ an integrated training paradigm that combines supervised fine-tuning for a cold-start, followed by a targeted reinforcement learning phase to cultivate dimension-specific expert models, which are then consolidated into a single unified model via weight merging. Experimental results demonstrate that SpeechReward significantly outperforms existing open-source and proprietary models in speech assessment capabilities and provides superior interpretability through its CoT-based explanations. More importantly, SpeechReward establishes a new, robust paradigm for automated speech evaluation and provides actionable metrics that can be used for the granular fine-tuning of next-generation TTS systems.
